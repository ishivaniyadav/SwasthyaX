Available Diseases

Flu

The flu is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses. Symptoms include fever, chills, and fatigue.

Cold

The common cold is a viral infection affecting the nose and throat, leading to sneezing, runny nose, and sore throat.

Allergy

Allergies occur when your immune system reacts to substances like pollen or food, causing sneezing or skin irritation.

Covid-19

Covid-19 is a respiratory illness caused by the coronavirus SARS-CoV-2. Symptoms may include fever, cough, and shortness of breath.

Gastroenteritis

Gastroenteritis is inflammation of the stomach and intestines, leading to symptoms like diarrhea, vomiting, and stomach cramps.

Pneumonia

Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs, leading to symptoms like cough, chest pain, and difficulty breathing.

Asthma

Asthma is a condition in which the airways become inflamed and narrow, causing difficulty breathing, coughing, and wheezing.

Tuberculosis

Tuberculosis is a bacterial infection that primarily affects the lungs, causing symptoms like coughing, chest pain, and fatigue.

Liver Cirrhosis

Liver cirrhosis is the scarring of the liver tissue, often caused by long-term liver damage due to alcohol or hepatitis.

Diabetes

Diabetes is a condition where the body is unable to properly process glucose, leading to high blood sugar levels.

Heart Disease

Heart disease refers to a range of conditions affecting the heart, including coronary artery disease, heart failure, and arrhythmias.

Anemia

Anemia is a condition where you lack enough healthy red blood cells to carry adequate oxygen to your body's tissues.

Hypertension

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is a condition that can cause serious health problems like heart disease and stroke if left untreated.

Lung Cancer

Lung cancer begins in the lungs and is often caused by smoking. Symptoms include coughing, chest pain, and weight loss.

Celiac Disease

Celiac disease is an autoimmune condition triggered by consuming gluten, causing damage to the small intestine.

Chronic Bronchitis

Chronic bronchitis is long-term inflammation of the airways, causing cough, mucus production, and breathing difficulty.

Sinusitis

Sinusitis is the inflammation of the sinuses, causing congestion, headache, and facial pain or pressure.

Kidney Stones

Kidney stones are hard deposits of minerals that form in the kidneys, causing severe pain and urinary issues.

Urinary Tract Infection

UTI is an infection in any part of the urinary system, causing a burning sensation while urinating and frequent urges to urinate.

Influenza

Influenza, commonly known as the flu, is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses. Symptoms include fever, chills, cough, sore throat, and body aches.

Eczema

Eczema is a group of conditions that cause inflammation, redness, and itching of the skin. It can result from allergies, stress, or irritants in the environment.

Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition that causes the rapid growth of skin cells, resulting in scaling, redness, and irritation, often on the elbows, knees, and scalp.

Gout

Gout is a form of arthritis caused by the accumulation of uric acid crystals in the joints, leading to sudden and severe pain, especially in the big toe.

Hepatitis

Hepatitis refers to inflammation of the liver, which can be caused by viral infections (such as Hepatitis A, B, and C), alcohol use, or certain medications, leading to symptoms like jaundice and fatigue.

Stroke

A stroke occurs when blood flow to part of the brain is blocked or reduced, resulting in brain cell death and causing symptoms like weakness, numbness, confusion, and difficulty speaking or seeing.
